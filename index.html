TL;DR

We investigate whether checklists should be used for all questions or selectively, generate them using six methods, evaluate their effectiveness across eight model sizes, and identify checklist items that correlate with human evaluations.

Our Method

Checklist Generation

  • We create three methods (control checklist length, more specified generation, and self-refine) in addition to the baseline and existing method.

    • Evaluation Responses

      • We evaluate a total of eight models, including gpt-4o-2024-08-06 and Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct to investigate which checklist items contribute to alignment with human evaluation.

Analysis

To evaluate the effectiveness of checklists generated by large language models (LLMs), we conducted a comprehensive analysis from multiple perspectives:

Bootstrapping: We applied bootstrap sampling to statistically test whether the checklist items that contribute to correlation with human evaluation are significant.

Ablation Study on Correlation with Human Evaluation: To identify which checklist items contribute to alignment with human scoring, we performed an ablation analysis, revealing both impactful and non-impactful items.

Analysis of Questions in the Dataset: We studied how checklist effectiveness depends on question type (closed vs. open).

Comparison with Human-Written Checklists: We compared LLM-generated checklists with those created by human annotators, analyzing overlaps and divergences in checklists.

Human Annotation of Generated Items: To identify checklist item characteristics affecting alignment with human evaluations, we conducted human annotations.

Analysis by Generation Method: We analyzed the characteristics of checklists produced by different generation policies to understand the strengths and limitations of each.
